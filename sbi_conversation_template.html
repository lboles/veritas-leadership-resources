SBI-C Conversation Planning Template

Structure Your Difficult Conversations for Success

About the SBI-C Model

The SBI-C model helps you have difficult conversations that are professional, productive, and focused on positive outcomes. By structuring your conversation around Situation, Behavior, Impact, and Commitment, you can address issues while maintaining relationships.

Use this template to plan your conversation before meeting with the individual.

Conversation Preparation

S Situation

Guidance: Describe the specific context when the issue occurred. Be factual and specific about time, place, and circumstances. Avoid generalizations like "always" or "never."

Good Example:

"During yesterday's team meeting when we were discussing the new curriculum changes..."

Avoid:

"You always interrupt in meetings" (too general, uses "always")

Good Example:

"I noticed you spoke over two colleagues and interrupted the presentation three times..."

Avoid:

"You were being disrespectful and rude" (interpretation, not observable behavior)

Guidance: Explain the effect the behavior hadâ€”on you, the team, students, or the organization. Connect to goals, values, and outcomes that matter.

Good Example:

"Which prevented us from hearing all perspectives and delayed our decision-making by 20 minutes..."

C Commitment

Guidance: Invite collaboration on solutions and agree on next steps. Focus on future behavior and mutual commitments. Make it specific and measurable when possible.

Good Example:

"Can we agree on a way to ensure everyone gets to share their thoughts in future meetings? What ideas do you have?"

Conversation Success Tips

  • Choose a private, comfortable setting
  • Start with a positive intention statement
  • Stay calm and professional throughout
  • Listen actively to their perspective
  • Be prepared to adjust your approach based on their response
  • End with clear next steps and timeline
  • Document the conversation and agreements afterward
