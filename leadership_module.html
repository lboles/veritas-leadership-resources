Lesson 1
The Anatomy of Conflict
Understanding workplace tension

Key Insight

"Conflict is inevitable ‚Äî how we handle it defines our leadership."

Every leader will face conflict. The difference between effective and ineffective leaders is not whether conflict occurs, but how they respond when it does.

Common Conflict Triggers

  • Unclear expectations: When roles or goals are ambiguous
  • Limited resources: Competition for budget, time, or personnel
  • Communication gaps: Misunderstandings and poor information sharing
  • Power struggles: Disputes over authority and decision-making
  • Value clashes: Fundamental differences in beliefs and priorities

Types of Conflict

Task Conflict

Disagreements about processes and procedures. These can be productive when managed well.

Relationship Conflict

Emotional tension between individuals. These require careful attention to prevent escalation.

Leadership Etiquette & Presence
Building professional credibility

Core Principle

"How you show up speaks louder than what you say."

Your presence and behavior set the tone for every interaction and establish your credibility as a leader.

Three Elements of Executive Presence

1. Clarity

Clear communication, decisive thinking, and transparent intentions

2. Consistency

Reliable behavior, predictable responses, and steady leadership style

3. Composure

Emotional regulation, calm under pressure, and thoughtful responses

Professional Etiquette Examples

  • Preparedness: Always come to meetings ready with materials
  • Verbal tone: Maintain professional, respectful language
  • Email professionalism: Clear subject lines and timely responses
  • Virtual meetings: Professional background and active participation

Lesson 3
The SBI-C Model
Structured communication for difficult conversations

The SBI-C Framework

S - Situation

Describe the specific context when the issue occurred

B - Behavior

Explain the observable actions or words (facts, not interpretations)

I - Impact

Share the effect the behavior had on you, the team, or organization

C - Commitment

Agree on next steps and future expectations

SBI-C Example

Situation: "When you arrived late to yesterday's meeting..."

Behavior: "I noticed the team had to stop and recap..."

Impact: "Which delayed our decision-making..."

Commitment: "Can we agree on a plan for future meetings?"

Lesson 4
Conflict Resolution
Systematic approaches to resolving disputes

Interest-Based Problem Solving

Focus on interests, not positions. Collaborate toward win-win solutions.

4-Step Resolution Process

Step 1: Clarify the Problem

Ensure everyone understands the issue and agrees on what needs to be solved

Step 2: Identify Interests

Discover what each party really needs (not just what they're asking for)

Step 3: Brainstorm Options

Generate multiple potential solutions without judgment

Step 4: Agree on Solution

Select the best option and create a clear implementation plan

De-Escalation Phrases

  • "Let's take a moment to reset."
  • "I hear what you're saying."
  • "What solution do you see working?"
  • "Help me understand your perspective."

Lesson 5
High-Anxiety Environments
Creating psychological safety

Warning Signs

Watch for these indicators:
  • Constant emergency meetings
  • Emotional outbursts from staff
  • Silence from key team members
  • Increased sick days and turnover
  • Gossip and rumors spreading

Leadership's Role

  • Model calm responses: Your emotional state influences others
  • Reinforce psychological safety: Make it safe to voice concerns
  • Set clear boundaries: Reduce ambiguity that creates anxiety
  • Communicate frequently: Information reduces fear and speculation

Prevention Through Culture

Prevention happens by building positive culture ‚Äî not waiting for crisis.
Proactive culture building includes regular check-ins, clear communication channels, professional development, and recognition programs.
